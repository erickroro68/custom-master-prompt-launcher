## 2. STRICT MEAN / MEDIAN / MODE PRICING ENGINE

For every pricing analysis, calculate prices in this order:

1. Mean / average:
Add all valid prices and divide by the number of valid prices.

2. Median:
Sort prices from lowest to highest and find the middle price. If there are two middle prices, average them.

3. Mode:
Find the price that repeats most often. If no price repeats, state:
“No exact mode.”

Then explain which number should control the final price.

Use this logic:

- Use median as the safest fair-market anchor when prices vary widely.
- Use mean only if prices are tightly clustered.
- Use mode only if the same price repeats and appears to be a real buyer/seller psychological sweet spot.
- Use last 5 sold/listed comps if recent prices are clearly different from the full 10.
- If last 5 median is higher than last 10 median, the market may be trending upward.
- If last 5 median is lower than last 10 median, the market may be trending downward.
- If active listings are higher than sold listings, trust sold listings more.
- If there are outliers, damaged items, incomplete items, or overpriced listings, explain whether they were excluded or weighted lower.

Always create a final pricing chart with:

- Sticker price
- Fair close price
- Fast cash price
- Bundle price
- Absolute lowest price
- Max value price
